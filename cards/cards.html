Mountains

Mountain biomes are diverse, high-altitude environments defined by steep slopes, varying temperatures, high winds, and intense UV.

Desert

Deserts are extremely dry environments that are home to well-adapted plants and animals. The main types of deserts include hot and dry deserts, semi-arid deserts, coastal deserts, and cold deserts.

Forest

There are three general types of forest that exist: temperate, tropical, and boreal. Experts estimate that these forests cover approximately one-third of Earth's surface.

Savannah

The savanna biome consists of areas of open grassland with very few trees. Biomes are defined by their unique vegetation and animal life, and the savanna is a type of grassland biome.

Sea

The ocean is a vast and dynamic ecosystem that covers over 70% of the Earth's surface. It is home to a wide range of organisms, from tiny plankton to massive whales.

Snow

A snowy mountains biome, prominent in games like Minecraft, represents high-altitude, cold regions with coniferous trees (spruce, pine), deep snow, and unique mobs like goats, foxes, and polar bears

Taiga

The taiga, or boreal forest, is the largest terrestrial biome, characterized by long, cold winters and short, mild summers. It is dominated by coniferous trees like pine, spruce, and fir, and features thin, acidic soil and wildlife such as moose, bears, wolves, and lynxes.

Tundra

The tundra is Earth's coldest biome, defined by long, harsh winters, low precipitation (like a desert), and permafrost (frozen soil) that prevents deep root growth, creating treeless plains with low-growing plants, mosses, and lichens.
